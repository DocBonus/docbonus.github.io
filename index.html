Knot Theory · Machine Learning · Purdue University

Predicting Knot Invariants via Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks

This work determined the learnability of knot invariants using neural networks. We trained a Kolmogorov–Arnold Network (KAN) to predict the Bridge Number of a knot directly from its Gauss code.

Conducted through the Purdue Experimental Mathematics Laboratory with Anand Shanker (Purdue CS & Math), Benjamin Mudrak, under the supervision of Prof. Thi Hanh Vo and program coordinator Prof. Thomas Sinclair.

Universal Algebra · Independent Research

Identities of the Graph Variety Axiomatized by x²y ≈ xy²

A characterisation of the graph variety ModG{x²y ≈ xy²} — the class of all directed graphs whose graph algebras satisfy the term equation x²y ≈ xy². Two main results are established: a structural theorem characterising membership via loop-uniformity within connected components, and an identity theorem giving necessary and sufficient conditions for a term equation to be an identity of the variety.

Joint work with Tiang Poomsa-ard. Presented March 2024.
